# UK Mansion Tax Analysis - 2024 Data
## Sales-Based Constituency Analysis

**Analysis Date:** November 21, 2025
**Data Source:** UK Land Registry Price Paid Data (2024) + ONS NSPL (February 2025)
**Geographic Coverage:** England, Wales, Scotland (98.3% match rate)

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines the distribution and revenue impact of the proposed UK mansion tax using **all 2024 property transactions** (881,757 sales) mapped to Westminster Parliamentary constituencies.

The mansion tax proposal would add a council tax surcharge on properties valued above £1.5 million or £2 million (threshold under debate), targeting the top 3 council tax bands (F, G, H).

## Methodology

### Data Sources

**1. Property Transactions**
- UK Land Registry Price Paid Data (2024)
- Full year: January - December 2024
- Total: 881,757 transactions across England, Wales, Scotland

**2. Geographic Mapping**
- ONS National Statistics Postcode Lookup (February 2025)
- Westminster Parliamentary Constituencies (2024 boundaries)
- 2,701,371 active postcodes mapped

**3. Constituency Names**
- ONS Westminster Parliamentary Constituency lookup
- 650 constituencies (2024 boundaries effective from July 2024 election)

### Analysis Process

1. **Data Loading**
   - Loaded all 2024 Land Registry transactions (881,757)
   - Filtered for properties above £1.5m and £2m thresholds

2. **Geographic Matching**
   - Matched property postcodes to NSPL data
   - Linked postcodes to constituency codes
   - Mapped constituency codes to names
   - **Match rate: 98.3%** (excellent coverage)

3. **Constituency Aggregation**
   - Counted sales per constituency
   - Calculated price statistics (mean, median)
   - Estimated annual revenue at £2,000 per property

4. **Analysis & Reporting**
   - Ranked constituencies by impact
   - Analyzed geographic distribution
   - Calculated concentration metrics

### Revenue Calculation

**Simplified Model:**
- Flat £2,000 annual surcharge per property (from FT article)
- Based on actual 2024 sales above thresholds
- Does not account for:
  - Variation in surcharge by property value
  - Deferral option uptake
  - Total housing stock vs. sales

## Important Limitations

### 1. Sales vs. Housing Stock

**This analysis uses property SALES, not total housing STOCK.**

- 2024 sales: 14,581 above £1.5m / 8,328 above £2m
- FT estimate: 145,000-300,000 total properties affected
- **Sales represent ~5-10% of total stock** (assuming 5-7% annual turnover)

**Implication:** Revenue estimates here are based on one year of sales. True policy impact requires full housing stock data.

### 2. Partial Year Analysis

- 2024 is a complete year (unlike 2025 which has partial data)
- Represents full market cycle
- No seasonal adjustment needed

### 3. Council Tax Band Data Not Used

- Mansion tax targets bands F, G, H specifically
- This analysis uses price thresholds only
- Some properties above thresholds might be in wrong bands (won't be taxed)
- Some properties in F/G/H bands might be below thresholds (won't be taxed)

### 4. Simplified Revenue Model

- Uses flat £2,000 surcharge (FT article average)
- Actual surcharge would vary by:
  - Amount above threshold
  - Property value
  - Council tax band
- Doesn't model deferral option

### 5. Behavioral Responses Not Modeled

- Market price adjustments
- Owner reactions (sell, defer, restructure)
- Impact on transaction volumes
